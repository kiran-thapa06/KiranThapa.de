Chandragiri Temple, located on Chandragiri Hill about 16 km southwest of Kathmandu, is a revered Hindu site dedicated to Lord Shiva. Known as the Bhaleshwor Mahadev Temple, it is believed to grant the wishes of devotees. Perched at an altitude of 2,551 meters, the temple offers stunning panoramic views of the Himalayas, including peaks like Mt. Everest, Langtang, and Ganesh Himal, along with a picturesque view of the Kathmandu Valley.
The site is easily accessible via a scenic cable car ride from Thankot, making it a popular destination for both pilgrims and tourists. Historically, Chandragiri is linked to King Prithvi Narayan Shah, who prayed here for strength during his campaign to unify Nepal.
